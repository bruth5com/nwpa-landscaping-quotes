Finishing the Job After Tree Removal

A stump left behind after tree removal or land clearing is more than an eyesore, it gets in the way of mowing, replanting and finishing the job properly. Grinding it below grade clears the way to move forward with your yard or property.

What's Included

  • Stump grinding below grade
  • Single stumps or multiple stumps from a clearing job
  • Grindings left for fill or hauled away, your choice
  • Access-friendly equipment for tight yards
  • Follow-up service after tree removal or land clearing jobs
  • Free on-site assessment

Why Grind Instead of Leave It

  • Stumps left in place can resprout or attract pests
  • Mowing around stumps is a constant hassle
  • Grinding clears the way to replant grass, trees or landscaping
  • Finishes the job after tree removal or land clearing

Local note: Stump grinding is commonly requested as a follow-up after storm damage tree removal or a larger land clearing job anywhere across the seven counties.

How the Process Works

  • Free on-site assessment of the stump or stumps
  • Straightforward quote based on number and size
  • Grinding scheduled around your timeline
  • Grindings left as fill or hauled away, your choice

Common Questions

Can multiple stumps be ground in one visit?

Yes, crews commonly grind multiple stumps in a single visit, especially after a larger tree removal or clearing job.

What happens to the wood chips from grinding?

You can keep them as mulch or fill, or have the crew haul them away — just let them know your preference when you request your estimate.

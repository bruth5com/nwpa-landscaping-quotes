Reclaiming Overgrown Land or Prepping to Build

Whether you're reclaiming a lot that's grown wild for years or clearing a site ahead of a new shed, garage or build, land clearing takes the right equipment and know-how to do it without creating a bigger mess than you started with.

What's Included

  • Brush and undergrowth clearing
  • Small tree and sapling removal
  • Lot clearing for sheds, garages or new construction
  • Debris hauled off or mulched on site
  • Skid steer and forestry mulching equipment available
  • Free on-site assessment

Common Reasons Homeowners Clear Land

  • Reclaiming an overgrown lot or field
  • Prepping a building site before construction
  • Opening up access for a driveway or trail
  • Clearing around a camp or cabin

Local note: Wooded acreage is common across NW Pennsylvania rural properties. On larger jobs, crews will coordinate utility locating before any digging or clearing begins near buried lines.

How the Process Works

  • Free assessment of the land and what you're trying to accomplish
  • Straightforward quote based on acreage and density of growth
  • Clearing scheduled around your timeline
  • Debris left as mulch or hauled away, your choice

Common Questions

Do you handle tree removal as part of clearing?

Small to mid-size tree removal is typically included; larger tree removal may be quoted separately depending on size and access.

Can you clear a site to prep for construction?

Yes, many crews clear and grade lots specifically to prep for sheds, garages and new builds.

Keeping Fields and Fence Lines Under Control

Overgrown fields and pastures reclaim themselves fast across NW Pennsylvania once mowing stops. Brush hogging cuts back brush, saplings and tall growth that regular mowers can't handle, whether it's a single overgrown acre or a full working field.

What's Included

  • Rotary mowing for fields, pastures and overgrown lots
  • Fence line and property line clearing
  • One-time cleanup or recurring seasonal mowing
  • Equipment sized to the job, from compact tractors to heavy-duty brush hogs
  • Debris left mulched in place or hauled off, your choice
  • Free on-site assessment before any work begins

Signs You Need Brush Hogging

  • Field or pasture overgrown with brush and saplings
  • Fence lines disappearing into growth
  • Property boundary hard to see or access
  • Land sitting unused because it's grown in

Local note: Rural acreage across Venango, Crawford, Mercer and Butler Counties can reclaim itself in a single growing season. Fields mowed before fall stay easier to maintain going into next spring.
